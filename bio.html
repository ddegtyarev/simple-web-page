Mikhail Gulin

Conceptual Artist · Curator · Actionist

Biography

Bold color, conceptual rigor, and playful irony rooted in Belarusian reality.

Mikhail Gulin is a Belarusian conceptual artist and curator whose practice spans painting, sculptural objects, and performative actions. His work reimagines familiar pop culture icons and Western art imagery through the prism of Eastern European identity, often contrasting vibrant color with sharp social commentary.

He graduated from the Belarusian State Academy of Arts in 2004 and joined the Union of Artists of Belarus in 2005. Gulin has curated landmark projects such as Palace Complex (Belarus, 2012) and Space of Diffusion (Russia, 2015; Georgia, 2017), foregrounding cross-border dialogue and experimental formats.

Exhibited internationally—from the 6th Moscow Biennale of Contemporary Art to independent spaces in Poland, Germany, Ukraine, and Lithuania—his projects explore solidarity, resistance, and the elasticity of cultural symbols.
