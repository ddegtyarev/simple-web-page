Mikhail Gulin

Conceptual Artist | Curator | Actionist

Mikhail Gulin is a Belarusian conceptual artist, curator, and actionist whose work spans painting, objects, and performances. His art engages with socio-political themes, reimagining pop culture and Western art through the lens of Belarusian realities. Using vibrant colors, iconic cultural references, and irony, he explores social stereotypes and contemporary issues.

A graduate of the Belarusian State Academy of Arts (2004), Mikhail has been a member of the Union of Artists of Belarus since 2005. He has curated notable international projects, including Palace Complex (Belarus, 2012) and Space of Diffusion (Russia, 2015; Georgia, 2017). His work has been featured in exhibitions such as the Belarusian Pavilion at the 53rd Venice Biennale (2009), the Moscow Biennale (2015), and other prominent showcases across Europe.
